====== pannakikamalala, mannakikamalala ======

===== Kaipapanan: =====

Ti pannakikamalala ket nu ti naasawaan ket makinaig ti saan na nga asawa.  Ti "mannakikamalala" iladawan na ti tao nga nakabasol.

  * Ti "mannakikamalala" iladawan na ti tao nga nakabasol ti pannakikamalala.
  * Ti pannakikamalala pardien na ti karing-kari dagiti agasawa nga inkari da tunggal maysa idi nagkasar da.
  * Binilin ti Dios dagiti taga Israel nga saan da nga makikamalala.
  * Ti "mannakikamalala" ket inaramat nga pangngarig tapno iladawan na dagiti tattao ti Israel a saan nga napudno iti Dios, kangrunaan nu agrukbab da kadagiti didiosen.

===== Translation Suggestions: =====

  * If the target language does not have one word that means "adultery," this term could be translated with a phrase such as, "having sexual relations with someone else's wife."
  * Some languages may have an indirect way of talking about adultery, such as "sleeping with someone else's spouse" or "being unfaithful to one's wife." (See: [[:ilo:ta:vol2:translate:figs_euphemism|euphemism]])
  * When "adulterous" is used in a figurative sense, it is best to translate it literally in order to communicate God's view of his disobedient people as being compared to an unfaithful spouse. If this is not acceptable in the language, the figurative use of this term could possibly be translated as "unfaithful" or "immoral" or "traiterous."
