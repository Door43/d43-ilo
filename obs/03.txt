====== 3. The Flood ======

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-01.jpg?direct&}}

Many years later, many people were living in the world. They had become very wicked and violent. It became so bad that God decided to destroy the whole world with a huge flood.

Kalpasan ti nabayad nga panawen, agingga nga immadu dagiti tao ti lubong, adadda nga nagbalinda nga dakes. Isu nga kinuna ti Dios, "Pukawek iti lubong babaen iti dakkel nga layus.''

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-02.jpg?direct&}}

But Noah found grace in God’s eyes. He was the only righteous man among all the wicked people. God told Noah about the flood He was planning to send. He told Noah to build a huge boat called an ark.

Ngem adda maysa nga lalaki nga napudno iti dios. Ti nagan na ket Noe. Maysa isuna nga Dios ken Noe, "Noe paglayusek ti lubong. Isu nga agaramid ka ti dakkel nga daong."

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-03.jpg?direct&}}

God told Noah to make the boat about 140 meters long, 23 meters wide, and 13.5 meters high. It was to be made of wood and have three levels, many rooms, a roof, and a window. The boat would keep Noah, his family, and two of every kind of animal safe during the flood.

Kinuna ti Dios ken Noe, ''Ti sukat ti daong nga aramidem, maysa nga gasut ken uppat nga metro ti kaatigdog ,duapulo ket tallo metro ti akabana, ken sangapulo ket tallo ti kagudwa na iti kangato na. Kayo a bangka iti aramidem. Aramidem nga tallo a palapag adu nga kuarto maysa ti bubong na. daytati plano nga inted ti Dios ken Noe. Isu nga babaen ti dakkel nga daong maisalakan iti pamilya ni Noe ken amin nga klase iti ayup."

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-04.jpg?direct&}}

Noah obeyed God. He and his three sons built the boat just the way God had told them. It took many years to build the boat, because it was so big. Noah warned the people about the coming flood and told them to turn to God, but they just laughed at him.

Inaramid ni Noe iti bilin ti Dios kenkuana tinulongan dagiti tallo nga annak na nga lalaki tapno matungpal iti ipapaaramid ti Dios kenkuana. Habang nga ar-aramiden da ti daong kinuna ni Noe kadagii tao,' Adda sumangpet nga dakkel nga layos. Masapul nga dumawat iti pammakawan ti Dios,' ngem saan nga mamati dagiti tattao kenkuana. Gapu ta dakkel ti daong,manu nga tawen iti naglabas sakbay nalpas ti daong.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-05.jpg?direct&}}

Noah and his family also gathered enough food for themselves and the animals. When everything was ready, God told Noah it was time for him, his wife, his three sons, and their wives to get into the boat—eight people in all.

Binilin manen ti Dios ni Noe,'' Agurnong kayo iti adu nga makan para kadakayo ken para kadagiti ayup. Idi nakasaganan iti amin, kinuna ti Dios ken Noe,''Noe, sumrek kan iti daong kadwan ti asawan ken ti tallo nga annak mo ken ti asawa da.'' kasta ngpaylaeng nga daytaarud ti inaramid da walo da laeng nga simrek ti daong.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-06.jpg?direct&}}

God sent a male and a female of every animal and bird to Noah so they could go into the boat and be kept safe during the flood. God sent seven males and seven females of every type of animal which could be used for sacrifices. When they were all in the boat, God Himself closed the door.

pinaiyapan ti Dios ti daong ken Noe ti amin nga klase ti ayup babassit nga tumatayab,lalaki ken babae pinaiyapan na met ti pito nga lalaki ken pito nga babae amin nga klase ti ayup nga mabalin na nga idaton ken kuanna.idi nakastrek dan aminem,insirran ti Dios ti ridaw ti daong.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-07.jpg?direct&}}

Then it began to rain, and rain, and rain. It rained for forty days and forty nights without stopping! Water also came gushing up out of the earth. Everything in the whole world was covered with water, even the highest mountains.

Kalpasan ti innem nga aldaw, nagtudo iti napigsa. Saan nga nagsardeng iti tudo na ti uneg ti kuarenta nga aldaw ken kuarenta nga rabii saan laeng n ga dayta.adda pay danum nga aggapu iti uneg ti daga intero nga passet iti lubong limneg dagiti tuktok ti bantay.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-08.jpg?direct&}}

Everything that lived on the dry land died, except the people and animals who were in the boat. The boat floated on the water and everything inside the boat was kept safe.

Natay amin nga tao ken ayup.Ti nabati laeng nga nabbiag ket dagiti adda iti uneg ti daong ni Noe. Agtaptapaw ti daong ni Noe iti layus. Ti amin nga nakalugan idiay nakalasat da iti layus.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-09.jpg?direct&}}

After the rains stopped, the boat floated on the water for five months, and then one day the boat rested on the top of a mountain. But the world was still covered with water. After three more months, the tops of the mountains were visible.

Idin sumarding iti tudon, lima nga bulan pay sakbay bimmaba ti layus. Maysa aldaw,nakadanun iti daong ni Noe iti maysa nga nangato nga bantay. Ngem nangato paylaeng ti danum iti sabali nga lugar ti lubong. Tallo nga bulan pay ti naglabas sakbay nga nakita ni Noe dagiti tuktok ti bantay.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-10.jpg?direct&}}

After forty more days, Noah sent out a bird called a raven to see if the water had dried up. The raven flew back and forth looking for dry land, but it could not find any.

kalpasan manen ti uppat apulo nga aldaw nagpatayab ti maysa nga uwak ni Noe tapno maammuan na no nagmagan ti daga nagtayab ti uwak, ngem awnn ti nakita na nga namaga nga daga.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-11.jpg?direct&}}

Later Noah sent out a bird called a dove. But it also could not find any dry land, so it came back to Noah. A week later he sent the dove out again, and it came back with an olive branch in its beak! The water was going down, and the plants were growing again!

saan nga nagbayad nagpatayab manen ti maysa nga kalapati ni Noe dagus nga nagsubli daytoy ken Noe gapu ta awan nakita na nga sanga ti kayo nga mabalin na nga bumabaan. Naglabas ti pito nga aldaw, nagpatayab manen ni Noe ti kalapati saan nga nagbayad,akan ni Noe nagsubli nga adda ti naalana nga bulong ti kayo.naragsakan ni Noe gapu ta naammuan nan nga bimmaban ti danum ken nagtutubo manen dagiti mula.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-12.jpg?direct&}}

Noah waited another week and sent out the dove a third time. This time, it did not come back. The water had dried up!

Nagpalabas manen ti maysa nga domingo ni Noe ket nagpatayab manen isuna ti kalapati.saanen nga nagsubli ti kalapati. Isu nga napanunot ni Noe,'' addan siguron iti bimmabaan nan.'' Kayat na nga satwen nga mabiiten nga agmaga ti daga.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-13.jpg?direct&}}

Two months later God said to Noah, “You and your family and all the animals may leave the boat now. Have many children and grandchildren and fill the earth.” So they obeyed and left the boat.

Kalpasan ti duwa nga bulan, kinuna ti Dios ken Noe,''mabalin kayon nga rummuar dita daong. Parwarem metten ti pamilyam ken amin nga ayup. Punnuen yo ti tao ken ayup ti lubong rimmuar da ngarud ti daong da Noe.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-14.jpg?direct&}}

After he got off the boat, Noah built an altar and sacrificed some of each kind of animal which could be used for a sacrifice. God was happy with the sacrifice and blessed Noah and his family.

pagkaruar da Noe ti daong ,nagaramid ti maysa nga altar. Nagidaton kadagiti ayup nga nasayaat nga idaton ti Dios naay-ayo ti Dios kadagiti indaton ni Noe. Isu nga benendissionan na ni Noe ken ti pamilyana.

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-15.jpg?direct&}}

God said, “I will never again curse the ground because of humans or destroy the world with a flood, even though humans are sinful from their youth.”

Kinuna ti Dios ken Noe,''Noe ikarek kadakayo saan kun nga uliten nga layusen ti lubong saan kun nga pukawen ti lubong babaen ti layus uray managbasol dagiti tao,''

{{https://api.unfoldingword.org/obs/jpg/1/en/360px/obs-en-03-16.jpg?direct&}}

God then made the first rainbow as a sign of His promise. Every time the rainbow appeared in the sky, God would remember what He promised and so would His people.

Kalpasan, na nangipakita ti Dios iti bulalayaw ken Noe tanda ti inkari. Isu nga kada agparang iti bulalayaw, malagip ti Dios ken dagiti tao ti inkarina.

//A Bible story from: Genesis 6-8//
